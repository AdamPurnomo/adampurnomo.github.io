About Me


Contact :

I am a graduate student at Tohoku University working at Neuro-Robotics Lab under the supervision of Prof. Mitsuhiro Hayashibe. I am interested in leveraging machine learning techniques for dynamical system modeling, robotics control and decision making. I am specifically developing a method to obtain the Lagrangian of mechanical systems from noisy measurement data with sparse regression methods.

I also have interests in computer vision, robotic perception, and manipulation. During my undergrad research, I was working on the application of Deep Learning for industrial bin-picking under the supervision of Prof. Kazuhiro Kosuge and Assoc. Prof. Shogo Arai.

Outside my academic life, I enjoy exercising very much, particularly running and weight lifting. Those two activities help me to come up with new insights whenever I am stuck with my research.

This website is a summary of research projects and other side projects that I have done. If you have any questions about my past projects, please don't hesitate to reach me out.
